Tally
$172M raised
"The first app to automate and manage your credit card debt"
What They Did
Tally was a "robo-advisor for debt." Users linked their credit cards, and Tally automatically made payments using an algorithm that prioritized highest-APR cards first. It extended users a single lower-interest line of credit (7.9%-29.9% APR vs typical 20%+ credit cards), consolidated their debt, and handled all payments. Users made one monthly payment to Tally instead of juggling multiple cards.
Unique Selling Proposition
Full automation: "First company to make full debt automation possible" - you literally do nothing after setup. Late fee protection: If you forgot to pay Tally, they'd still pay your cards to prevent fees. Revenue alignment: Tally only made money when their APR was LOWER than your cards - aligned incentives. No fees: No annual, balance transfer, late, or prepayment fees.

Tier 2: Strong PMF, Moderate AI Opportunity

Braid
$10M raised
"Shared wallets for groups - your money, our money"
What They Did
Braid created shared digital wallets ("money pools") for groups. Each pool had its own account/routing number, virtual cards, and debit card. Designed for roommates sharing rent, couples managing household expenses, parents with kids, or friends saving for trips together. Contributors didn't need Braid accounts - just send money to the pool.
Unique Selling Proposition
"Our money" vs "my money": Unlike Venmo (splitting after the fact), Braid was designed for ongoing shared expenses - the money lives together. Real bank accounts: Unique routing numbers, not just app balance. Spending controls: Limit individual spending, view history, assign card access. Free: Revenue from interchange only.
Why It Failed
Sponsor bank issues froze operations mid-2022. Had to migrate to another bank, never recovered momentum. Founders said relying on third-party software was the biggest mistake. Shut down Sept 2023, now building "Pool" as successor.
AI Rebuild Opportunity
Product was validated - failure was infrastructure, not market. AI could add: smart splitting suggestions, spending insights, proactive alerts. Fintech-as-a-service is more mature now (Unit, Treasury Prime). Lower risk to rebuild with better infra partners.

Fast
$120M raised
"One-click checkout for the entire web"
What They Did
Fast wanted to be your "shopping account for the web." Create an account once, then one-click purchase on any partner site - like Amazon's 1-click but universal. They tracked you across sites using cookies/IP/email. Also offered batch ordering (combine multiple transactions), zero-fraud guarantee for sellers, and didn't sell customer data to advertisers.
Unique Selling Proposition
True one-click: While competitors took 2+ steps, Fast completed orders immediately after clicking the button. Cross-platform identity: Works on any browser, device, platform. Privacy-first: Didn't funnel data to advertisers unlike competitors. Seller protection: Zero fraud guarantee on chargebacks.
Why It Failed
Generated only ~$600K revenue despite hundreds of employees and high-paid executives. Unsustainable burn rate. Shut down April 2022 - "stunning collapse" per TechCrunch.
AI Rebuild Opportunity
The product concept is solid - checkout friction kills conversions. Fast failed on execution/burn, not product-market fit. A lean AI-native team could build this with 1/10th the headcount. AI handles fraud detection, customer support, merchant onboarding.

Fuzzy
$75M raised
"24/7 pet telehealth - talk to a vet anytime"
What They Did
Fuzzy was the "first digitally native pet health provider." Users could snap a photo of their sick pet and get triaged by one of 65 veterinarians via 24/7 live chat. Also offered: ship-to-home prescriptions, vet-curated e-commerce, and educational content. $15/month subscription. Positioned as "first stop" before expensive in-person vet visits.
Unique Selling Proposition
Always available: 24/7 access - no waiting for vet appointments. Triage model: Helps determine if you NEED an in-person visit, saving money on unnecessary trips. Full ecosystem: Advice + prescriptions + products in one app. Cost savings: Claimed to save pet parents $750/year on healthcare.
Why It Failed
Abrupt closure with minimal warning - even executives reportedly didn't know timing. Allegations of unpaid debts to creditors, vendors, and employees. Pivoted multiple times (cut 70% of team at one point).
AI Rebuild Opportunity
Pet telehealth is a huge market (COVID accelerated adoption). AI can handle initial triage, symptom assessment, and basic advice - escalating to human vets only for complex cases. 80% of queries could be AI-handled. Much lower cost structure.

Artifact
Undisclosed (Instagram founders)
"TikTok for news - AI-personalized reading"
What They Did
Created by Instagram co-founders Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. A personalized news app that used ML to recommend articles based on your reading behavior. "The feed improves every time you read." Features included: AI-rewritten clickbait headlines, AI-generated audio (including Snoop Dogg voice), article summaries in different styles ("Gen Z" or "Explain Like I'm Five"), and social sharing.
Unique Selling Proposition
Learning algorithm: Pick 10+ topics, read 25 articles, feed gets smarter. Anti-clickbait: Users could flag clickbait headlines, AI would rewrite them. Quality control: Only included publishers meeting editorial standards. Challenge your views: Algorithm designed to surface content that might challenge previously held views.
Why It Failed
Low user engagement - 100K downloads at launch, dropped to 12K new installs by October 2023. Feature sprawl diluted core value (added places, social posting, etc.). Founders concluded market wasn't big enough. Shut down January 2024, but Systrom says they're "continuing to keep it alive."
AI Rebuild Opportunity
The product concept had merit but execution wandered. A focused rebuild could: stick to core news personalization, add better AI summaries/discussion, integrate with workflow tools. News consumption habits have shifted - might be right time with better AI.

Eaze

"Uber for weed - cannabis delivery in minutes"
What They Did
Eaze connected users with licensed cannabis dispensaries for on-demand delivery. Browse products (flowers, edibles, vapes, concentrates), add to cart, pay, track delivery.
